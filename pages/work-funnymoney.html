FunnyMoney

Social Budgeting for Gen Z Students

A social-first financial wellness platform that reframes budgeting as a gamified, emotionally aware, community-driven experience — helping Gen Z students manage spending without sacrificing their social lives. Winner of the 2026 SIGCHI Design Jam.

Problem

Gen Z students understand that financial planning matters, but consistently disengage from budgeting tools that feel corporate, intimidating, and restrictive. Traditional apps overwhelm users with charts and treat spending as a problem — not a lifestyle.

Goal

Reframe budgeting as a socially connected lifestyle system — emotionally intelligent, passively tracked, and gamified — so financial awareness becomes motivating rather than anxiety-inducing.

Role

UX Research · UX Strategy · Product Design · Research Synthesis

Research Methods
Semi-Structured Interviews Survey (n = 45) Affinity Diagramming Behavioral Research Mixed Methods
Interview Research

Semi-structured interviews explored how students currently manage money, why budgeting tools fail to retain engagement, and what motivates long-term app usage.

Financial Behaviors
  • How do you currently track or manage your finances?
  • What is the biggest deterrent for young people learning about finance?
  • How do you budget for hobbies or leisure activities?
App Engagement
  • What is your most-used app or game right now?
  • What feature keeps you coming back?
  • What makes educational apps feel overwhelming?
Social Behaviors
  • How do you typically meet new people?
  • What challenges do you face socially?
  • Do you plan activities in advance or spontaneously?
Survey Research  ·  n = 45
  • Most students relied on "mental budgeting" rather than structured tracking systems
  • The majority checked their bank balance immediately before making a purchase
  • Flexible budgeting was strongly preferred over rigid financial structures
  • Gamified progress and social accountability outperformed traditional finance education as motivators
  • Cost anxiety significantly impacted social participation and event attendance
Research Synthesis
Financial Behavior
  • Users tracked spending mentally instead of formally
  • Budgeting felt associated with "adulting pressure"
  • Spending patterns shifted heavily based on hobbies and lifestyle
  • Many avoided budgeting apps entirely
Social Behavior
  • Leisure activities were treated as emotional rewards
  • Students prioritized experiences and social connection over savings
  • Cost barriers prevented participation in social events
  • Digital platforms served as the primary social connector
Gamification & Motivation
  • Progress systems, customization, and social motivation resonated strongly
  • Seeing friends make progress increased personal motivation
  • Information overload and dense financial terminology were immediate turn-offs
  • Users wanted achievable goals over complex data visualizations
Design Opportunity

Students did not want a strict budgeting tool, another spreadsheet, or a finance lecture. They wanted financial awareness without guilt, social accountability, personalized guidance, and motivation that felt emotionally rewarding. This led us to reframe budgeting as a socially connected lifestyle system rather than a restrictive finance tracker.

Product Concept

The platform automatically tracks spending and transforms transactions into personalized insights — without punishing users for spending money.

  • Passive transaction ingestion via secure bank API — no manual logging
  • AI-generated financial reflections with conversational, emotionally aware tone
  • Gamified progress systems with small wins and achievable goals
  • Social accountability features tied to friends, shared goals, and visible progress
  • Adaptive budgeting habits that learn from user corrections over time
Technical Feasibility

The platform connects to a user's bank account using financial aggregation APIs, providing secure read-only access with tokenized authentication. The app never stores banking credentials.

AI Transaction Pipeline
Ingest → Clean

Plaid API pulls raw transaction data. Plaid Enrich normalizes merchant names and categories.

Analyze → Refine

Gemini API applies behavioral labeling and tone generation. Users can override labels to correct categorization.

Learn

The system stores user corrections in a personal database to improve future categorization automatically.

Example

Raw: "AMZN MKTP US $22.40" → Enriched: Shopping → AI label: "Impulse Buy" → User corrects to "Business Expense" → System remembers for next purchase.

Technology Stack
Node.js / Python PostgreSQL Firebase Cloud Messaging Gemini API Plaid API Cloud Infrastructure
Key UX Decisions
Emotional Tone Over Shame

Replaced aggressive warnings and guilt-based messaging with conversational AI feedback — humor and light emotional awareness instead of financial lectures.

Passive Tracking

Automatic transaction ingestion removed the friction of manual logging — the biggest reason users abandon budgeting apps.

Social Accountability

Budgeting became significantly more motivating when tied to friends, shared goals, and visible community progress.

Gamified Learning

Avoided dense dashboards and spreadsheet interfaces entirely. The experience centered on small wins, progress indicators, and personalized encouragement.
